    The History of Lucky Paper Stars

    What Are Lucky Paper Stars?

    Lucky paper stars, also known as Japanese lucky origami stars or wishing stars, are 3D stars folded from paper strips, often given as a gift to symbolize good luck and well wishes. Legend says that a Japanese girl named Hoshi started folding paper stars to save falling stars, and the practice spread, with the belief that one paper star saved one falling star. People also give them as tokens of love, admiration, and support, and a large collection of stars can be used to make a wish.
