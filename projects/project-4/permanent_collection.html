Permanent Collection

•••

"Midnight River"

Harold Benson (American, 19th-20th)

A moody watercolor painted during the height of the Hudson River School movement. Benson's bold rendering hints at other influences including Romantic traditions and Impressionism.

"Ceremonial Vessel"

Unknown Artisan (Pre-Columbian)

This ancient vessel, decorated with geometric patterns and symbolic imagery, represents a high point of technical and artistic achievement in Pre-Columbian cultures.

"Cityscape in Motion"

Sophia Whitmore (Mexican American, b. 1958)

Bold brushstrokes, expressionist color, and dynamic composition reflect the artist's exploration of urban movement and the energy of contemporary city life.

"Aurora Rising"

Department of Sculpture (Contemporary)

Commissioned specifically for the Aurora Art Museum, this abstract sculptural work features elegant curves and towering forms. Part of the permanent collection.

"Threads of Silence"

Mira D'Silva (Japanese, b. 1971)

Using traditional indigo dye and contemporary conceptual practice, D'Silva creates layered textile works that explore the intersection of craft and fine art.

"Self Portrait with Window"

Jane Chen (British-Asian, b. 1982)

A new complex study utilizing both historical and contemporary techniques. A refined emotional interiority is present in the European water- paint paintings.

"Digital Landscape #4"

Ravi Kapoor (Indian, b. 1985)

Using algorithmic code and projection mapped onto sculptural elements, Kapoor creates immersive experiences that bridge traditional media through technological innovation.
