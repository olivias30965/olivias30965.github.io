Artists of Our City

Marcus Hollis (b. 1975, Aurora, USA)

Marcus Hollis is a visual artist based in Aurora who specializes in mixed-media work exploring themes of identity, community, and belonging. His practice draws heavily on his personal heritage and the stories of those around him, creating works that resonate deeply with local audiences. Hollis has been a fixture in the Aurora art scene for over two decades, contributing not only through his own artistic practice but also as a mentor to emerging artists.

Sofia Romero, immigrant-based and cultural facilitator, Aurora

Sofia Romero is a multidisciplinary artist rooted in Aurora's vibrant immigrant communities. Her work is often site-specific, utilizing materials found in ordinary households and public spaces to create striking installations. Through her art, Romero explores the intersections of immigration, identity, and belonging, while also facilitating conversations about the transformative everyday materials into artistic expression.

Sofia has worked the traditions of her own artistic practice and is considered an artist and collaborator among diverse audiences within Aurora. Having dedicated herself to the meaningful conversations between artist and community, Sofia has brought new perspectives through the lens of immigrant experiences.

Caleb Nguyen (b. 1982, Aurora, USA)

Caleb Nguyen is a contemporary Aurora-based artist whose practice spans painting, printmaking, and conceptual installation. His artistic identity, bringing, and maturity. His works often blend Western contemporary forms with Vietnamese aesthetics, resulting in pieces that speak to both cultural heritage and the contemporary moment.

Nguyen has been exhibited in galleries in the country and has completed several public art commissions throughout the city. In a recent Nguyen is considered a leading figure in emerging contemporary art scene. Beyond his own artistic practice, he also teaches, sharing his skills and vision with the next generation of artists.
